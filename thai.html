Tom yum soup with prawns

Ingridents:

  • 200g onions, roughly chopped
  • 20g red chilli, sliced
  • 150g tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 3 tsp runny honey
  • 15g galangal, peeled and sliced
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 270g whole raw king prawns, peeled and deveined, heads removed and reserved
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, bashed
  • 120g oyster mushrooms, sliced
  • 8 lime leaves
  • 5 tsp fish sauce
  • 5 tsp lime juice
  • 70ml Thai coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp Thai chilli jam (optional)
  • small handful of coriander, roughly chopped (optional)

    • Method

    1. Pour 1.3 litres water into a large saucepan over a high heat. Add the onion, tomato, chilli, galangal, lemongrass, prawn heads and chicken stock cube. Stir and bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium and simmer for 20 mins until the liquid has reduced.

    2. Carefully strain the hot broth into a large heatproof bowl or jug, then discard the prawn heads. Return the strained veg and herb mixture to the saucepan and pour over the broth. Stir through the mushrooms and lime leaves, then cook for 3 mins until the mushrooms are tender.

    2.Add 1 tbsp sugar, the fish sauce, lime juice, coconut milk and prawns. Bring to the boil and cook until the prawns are cooked through, about 1-2 mins. Remove from the heat. Remove the lemongrass, then stir in the Thai chilli jam, if using. Scatter over the coriander to finish, if you like, and serve.



    Kimchi

    Ingridents:

  • 2-3 Chinese leaf (2kg prepared weight)
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 tbsp seaweed (I use wakame), lightly rinsed
  • 40-60g sea salt (3% of the cabbage weight)
  • ½ leek
  • 5g ginger, chopped
  • 40g garlic (10 cloves), peeled
  • 40g onion, chopped
  • 2 spring onions
  • 1 small pear, cored and chopped
  • 2-litre sterilised jar
  • 40g Korean chilli flakes (use less if you prefer it milder)

    • Method

    1.Chop the Chinese leaf into bite-size pieces, weighing it until you have 2kg, then wash under running water. Mix the Chinese leaf with the salt and the seaweed in a large bowl. Set aside.

    2.Every now and then, over the course of 3-4 hrs, mix the salted Chinese leaf and seaweed with your hands. (You will start to see liquid being released.) You want to be able to bend the Chinese leaf without breaking the pieces.

    3.Meanwhile, shred the carrot and leek, and chop the spring onions. Set aside. Make the chilli paste by blitzing the onion, garlic, ginger and pear in a food processor until puréed. Add the chilli flakes, then blitz again to combine. Drain the Chinese leaf mixture, removing as much water as you can. This may take about 10 mins.

    4.Toss the Chinese leaf mixture with the other vegetables, then mix in the chilli paste to coat everything. Tip into a 2-litre sterilised jar. Try not to have too many air pockets and leave a 1-inch space under the lid. Put a fermentation weight on top, or if you don’t have one, try using some baking beans in a bag. Keep a plate under the jar in case of overflow. After 24-48 hrs you will begin to see bubbles appearing. That means fermentation is underway.

    5.At any point during the fermentation, you can taste the kimchi to see how you like the flavour. I prefer to keep mine in the fridge after day 3 to slow down the process and start enjoying it. You can transfer the kimchi into smaller jars for easy access from the fridge. It also makes a great present for family and friends.



    Hoisin duck stir-fry

    Ingridents:

  • 1 tbsp groundnut oil
  • 2 skinless duck breasts (250g), sliced
  • 1 carrot, peeled, halved lengthways and finely sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 pak choi, quartered
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • 400g cooked rice or egg noodles
  • 3 spring onions, shredded
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 3 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • pinch of Chinese five spice
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar or rice vinegar
  • 1 celery stick, finely sliced

    • Method

    1.Heat a wok or a large frying pan over a high heat. Drizzle in 1/2 tbsp of the oil and stir-fry the duck for 5 mins until cooked through and golden. Set aside on a plate. Heat the remaining 1/2 tbsp oil and add the pak choi, onion, carrot and celery. Fry for another 5 mins until golden but still crunchy.

    2.Mix the vinegar, five spice, hoisin, 6 tbsp water, and cornflour together in a small bowl. Add to the wok with the veg along with the cooked duck. Stir well and simmer for 2-3 mins until the sauce thickens and coats the veg. Scatter with the spring onions and serve with noodles or rice and the lemon wedges for squeezing over.



    Hoisin sauce

    Ingridents:

  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • ⅛ tsp black pepper
  • hot sauce, to taste
  • 2 tsp sesame seed oil
  • 1 garlic clove, finely crushed
  • 2 tsp rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp dark brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp smooth peanut butter

    • Method

    1. Put all the ingredients into a food processor or jug and blitz with a stick blender until smooth. Will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to three weeks.
